Each sprint has a single goal. If a feature doesn't move the goal, it doesn't ship in that sprint. Effort estimates assume Ali working solo, evenings + weekends after RTB day job (~15-20 focused hours/week).

## Sprint 2 — First customer (3-4 weeks, the longest sprint)

**Goal:** Dr. Saif uses Velo daily for 30 consecutive days. Free trial.

This sprint contains less "code shipping" and more "watch Dr. Saif use the product." Most issues you discover here are not bugs — they're design assumptions you got wrong. Plan for it.

### Week 1 — Onboarding

- Onboard Dr. Saif's clinic via OperatorConsole
- Sit with him in his clinic for half a day — show him every feature, watch him use it
- Migrate his existing patient list (if any) — likely from paper or Excel; expect 50-300 records
- Set up his WhatsApp Business API integration
- Configure his clinic settings (currency: IQD, working hours, doctors list)

### Weeks 2-4 — Watch + iterate

- Daily check-in with Dr. Saif (WhatsApp message, 30s response)
- Weekly visit or video call (30-60 minutes)
- Maintain a running list of every friction point Dr. Saif mentions
- **Fix only blockers** during this sprint. Defer everything else to Sprint 3.

### Sprint 1 backlog items that get pulled in IF blockers

- **S1.5** Tasks DB persistence (if Dr. Saif uses tasks heavily)
- **S1.6** Goals DB persistence (if he uses goals)
- **S1.8** InboxPage refactor (if WhatsApp inbox flow has bugs)
- **S1.16** Calendar default doctor filter (if his receptionist hates the default)

### Sprint 2 close criteria:
- Dr. Saif has used Velo for 30 days
- Dr. Saif has volunteered explicit feedback on what's missing
- You've identified the next 3-5 features that matter to him
- You have data on actual usage patterns (queries/day, most-used pages, etc.)
- Dr. Saif says "yes" to converting from free to paid (whatever price you settle on)

**Tag at close:** `v0.5.0-first-customer`

## Sprint 3 — Customer-driven (3 weeks)

**Goal:** Build what Dr. Saif's feedback says is missing. Get one second customer.

The features in this sprint cannot be predicted today. They will come from Dr. Saif's actual usage. Likely candidates based on existing patterns:

- Appointment reminders via WhatsApp (24h before, 2h before)
- Recall system (patients overdue for cleaning)
- Treatment plan PDF export (Dr. Saif gives patients a printed estimate)
- Receipt/invoice PDF (Iraqi clinics typically give a paper receipt)
- X-ray / image attachments on patient profile
- AI Assistant productionized (real Anthropic key, used for patient communication summaries)
- Clinic-side analytics (charts Dr. Saif actually looks at)

**Acquisition during this sprint:** Dr. Saif is asked to refer ONE peer. That clinic gets a 60-day free trial. Onboard them in week 3 of this sprint.

**Sprint 3 close criteria:**
- 2 clinics paying or in active trial
- The product has at least one new feature that came directly from customer feedback
- You have decided pricing (revisit Sprint 2 hypothesis with real data)

**Tag at close:** `v0.6.0`

## Sprint 4 — Repeatable onboarding (3 weeks)

**Goal:** Onboard a third clinic without burning yourself out.

By now, the friction in onboarding is what limits scale. Polish the operator workflow:

- Operator can self-serve clinic creation in <30 minutes (currently maybe 2 hours including hand-holding)
- Pre-built clinic templates (configurations for common Iraqi dental clinic shapes — solo practice, 2-doctor, 3-chair)
- Automated test account → real account conversion flow
- Documentation: "How a new clinic gets started in Velo" written in Arabic, sharable PDF
- Billing: figure out how clinics actually pay you (bank transfer? cash? FIB? Payoneer for USD subs?)

**Sprint 4 close criteria:**
- 3 clinics paying
- Onboarding a clinic is a repeatable, documented process
- Monthly recurring revenue exceeds infrastructure costs (Supabase + Vercel + Anthropic) with margin

## Sprints 5-6 — Repeat the loop (next 2-3 months)

Goals shift to:

- Reach 5-10 paying clinics
- Identify the patterns: what feature requests keep coming up?
- Build the 3-5 features that >50% of customers ask for
- Start to think about the next axis of growth (more clinics in Erbil? Baghdad? Jordan?)
- Decision point at end of Sprint 6: stay solo, or hire/contract help?

---

## Months 7-12 — Direction depends on signals

The honest answer is: **your roadmap past Sprint 6 depends on what you learn in Sprints 1-6.** Don't plan it now. The data you don't have yet (which features matter, which clinics buy, what they pay, churn rates, support load) determines everything past month 6.

That said, plausible directions:

### Direction A — Deepen in Iraq

20-50 clinics in Iraq. Hire one technical person to share dev load. Scale through referrals + light marketing. Ali stays operator + sales.

### Direction B — Expand the SKU

Same Iraqi customers, but build adjacent tools:
- Patient marketing (campaigns, recall automation)
- Lab management (linked to treatment plans)
- Inventory + purchasing (with supplier integration)

This is the "more revenue per existing customer" path.

### Direction C — Geographic expansion

Take what worked in Iraq to KRG-adjacent markets: Jordan, Lebanon, parts of the Gulf where private dentistry runs on cash. Hardest path because each new market requires currency, language, market-fit work.

### Direction D — Stop scaling, run as a profitable side business

If 10-15 clinics generates a livable side income with low support load, that might be the right answer. Not every SaaS needs to be a unicorn. Iraqi dental clinic management is a real, defensible niche.

**Decide at Sprint 6, not before.**

## Velo

**Dental practice management software, built for Iraq.**

---

### The problem

Dental clinics in Iraq run on paper, Excel, and informal WhatsApp. They lose patients to forgotten follow-ups. They under-collect revenue from incomplete treatment plans. They burn receptionist time on manual scheduling. The Western alternatives (Curve, Dentrix) cost $200-500/month, don't speak Arabic, don't work with WhatsApp, and don't support IQD.

### What Velo does

Velo replaces the paper, the Excel, and the chaotic WhatsApp threads with a single dental-clinic system:

- **Patients** with full medical history, allergies, and visit history
- **Anatomically-accurate dental charts** with FDI numbering, tooth surfaces, and color-coded findings
- **Treatment plans** that track procedures from "proposed" through "completed and paid"
- **Multi-doctor scheduling** with conflict detection
- **WhatsApp-first communication** — appointment reminders, payment receipts, patient questions, all through the channel patients actually use
- **IQD + USD billing** — built for Iraqi cash dentistry
- **Bilingual EN/AR** — Arabic-first design, RTL-aware throughout
- **Inventory, finance, reports** — the operations a small clinic actually runs

### Who it's for

Independent Iraqi dental clinics. Solo practices, 2-doctor teams, 3-chair operations. Owner-dentists who do everything: clinical work + billing + marketing + supply ordering. Their receptionists. Their hygienists. Not hospital dental departments. Not multi-location chains. Not insurance-heavy markets.

### Why now

WhatsApp Business API + cloud SaaS + LLM-powered patient communication makes a $30-50/month dental product economically viable for the first time. The Iraqi private healthcare market is growing as the middle class expands. Western players ignore the market. Local competitors don't exist.

### How it's sold

Operator-managed, not self-serve. SupCod3 (the company behind Velo) provisions every clinic, configures their integrations, supports them directly. Clinics don't sign up — they get a phone call, a meeting, a setup, and a relationship. This is intentional: in Iraq, trust beats funnels.

### Status

Foundation shipped May 2026. First customer (Dr. Saif AlShaker, Erbil) onboarding in Sprint 2. Free trial converting to paid at 90 days. Target: 5 paying clinics within 6 months, 25-50 within 12 months.
